ABOUT US

Working towards a sustainable world

Ways4Water’s mission is to protect global water resources and mitigate anthropogenic effects on aquatic ecosystems.
We restore eutrophic and polluted water bodies by combining ecology, science, technology and art.

By 2030, there will be a 40% deficit in water supply compared to global water demand. This will put an immense stress on our water resources. Over 2 billion people already live in countries experiencing high water stress.

Only 6% of surface water in NRW is rated as “good” by the Ministerium für Klimaschutz, Umwelt, Landwirtschaft, Natur, und Verbraucherschutz des Landes Nordrhein-Westfalen.

Ways4Water offers a sustainable solution that can help combat the effects of waterway pollution and its impact on the environment and the downstream effects that waterway pollution has on climate change in general, like rising water temperatures, oceanic acidification, and the creation of oceanic dead zones.

Regenerating water quality

Floating gardens improve water quality by taking up excess nutrients from the water. In turn, this counteracts eutrophication and stabilizes the local ecology.

Increasing biodiversity

Floating gardens create ecosystems for pollinators, birds and fish. Flowers attract pollinators, birds find safe nesting places and fish can shelter between plant roots.

Beautifying urban water

Floating gardens can be an attractive addition to urban water bodies, increasing foot traffic for local businesses and raising awareness for water protection.

Improving climate in cities

Floating gardens not only create beautiful scenery in a concrete jungle, they also improve water and air quality while decreasing ambient city temperatures.

Renaturalizing banks

Waterways have been excessively modified for industrial purposes. Floating gardens are a great solution to renaturalize banks without construction efforts.

Water quality monitoring

Floating gardens from W4W offer the possibility of continuous water quality monitoring, providing data to guide effective strategies for water remediation.
